  • Civic Design Conference

    Making space for community knowledge-sharing in a distributed world

    Virtual, December 2021

    Staying connected and learning from your teammates on a distributed team or in a highly siloed organization can be hard. Written knowledge-bases (if you have them) can be dry or become out of date, and replicating hallway conversations where you can bump into new people or share stories (and commiserations!) with coworkers can feel out of reach in a virtual environment. Despite the obstacles, I’ve found that cultivating an engaged remote community and fostering peer-to-peer knowledge sharing is possible. This talk will cover real-world tactics I use at the US Digital Service to engage communities and empower people to be resources to each other, whether they’re in the same room or thousands of miles apart.

  • Lesbians Who Tech Debug 2020

    Build Your Own Documented & Tested Healthcare API in 30 Mins

    Virtual, 2020

    Entering the healthcare tech space with a more general web development background? Here’s a hands-on introduction to FHIR concepts and how to apply REST API concepts and OpenAPI (formerly known as Swagger) open source tools to building a healthcare API, complete with interactive documentation and tests. Come away with your own API and a better understanding of FHIR, OpenAPI, and the rich open source ecosystem and community supporting good API development and healthcare interoperability.

  • APIdays Global 2018

    Bridging the Gap: Applying Lessons from API products to Healthcare Enterprise Integrations

    Paris, December 2018

    Leading the integrations team at a fast growth startup that lives at the intersection of healthcare and social services, I have worked with clients on both the payor and provider sides of the industry. Each of these groups have different resources and approaches to technology and business, but both are struggling to adopt modern API practices. This talk covers what I wish I’d known before trying to build out an API program at a startup serving these enterprise customers with complex, highly customized, on-prem legacy IT systems.
